About Us

In fall 2015, the College of Engineering received a $22 million commitment from The Grainger Foundation of Lake Forest, Illinois, to establish the Grainger Fund for Undergraduate Education. Electrical engineering alumnus David Grainger (BSEE ’50) is chairman of The Grainger Foundation.

This funding helped establish the Grainger Engineering Design Innovation Laboratory—our makerspace—at UW-Madison. Housed in the Wendt engineering building, the makerspace is another addition to the suite of resources in the college of engineering that encourage and facilitate creativity and innovation. This “high-tech DIY laboratory” provides the tools and support for our engineering students, faculty and staff to invent, 3D print, create, collaborate and fabricate their ideas.

About #

COMMUNITY

Our mission is to create a sense of community within the makerspace to promote synergistic collaborations between students from all desciplines.

STUDENT EMPOWERMENT

We want students that do not generally enter a shop to feel empowered and comfortable within the makerspace and unleash their creativity.

PRODUCT DESIGN

The space, resources, and skilled help, will serve as the HUB for inventing and designing new products at UW Madison.
